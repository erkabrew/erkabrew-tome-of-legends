## Mechanics Considerations

For the adaptation of Champions to DND, I have followed a series of self established rules that will be constantly present in each of them.

- Every Champion is designed to be a formidable encounter. They are all balanced as Mini-Bosses or Main Bosses, meaning a minimum Challenge Rating of 10.
- I’m not just adapting the flavor or essence; I am adapting the actual ingame mechanics into DND terms.

I know that for adapting champions to D&D should only take the essence and be simple, but I wanted to include the champion's gameplay and represent it in D&D so that Dungeon Masters who want to use them can feel that, even without having fully read the stat block, they have an idea of how to use them just by having tried them directly in League of Legends.

If you plan to use one of these champions in one of your campaigns, remember that the atmosphere and their presentation are very important!

## Important Advise for you, DM!
This advertisement is for you, Dungeon Master! It is important to mention that, as you may have read in the Mechanics Considerations, Champions are incredibly strong and difficult to face. In the event that your players are going to face one, ensure they have good equipment and good synergy during combat, otherwise, things could get very complicated, potentially leading to a TPK.

Likewise, to ensure everyone’s enjoyment, keep in mind not to use the 100% of a Champion's capacity depending on how the encounter is unfolding. Start by omitting a multiattack, or perhaps by not using their Ultimate right away, so the combat feels like it scales over time rather than being unfair from second one.

Even so, as they say, every table is a world of its own, so obviously do whatever you find most convenient to ensure your table’s enjoyment!
